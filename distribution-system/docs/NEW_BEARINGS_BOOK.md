1. Strategic directions & design principles
-----------------------------------------
This project is delivery-mode neutral: it evaluates multiple conveyance options (vehicles, gravity tubes, robots, conveyors) against multi-objective optimization goals (waste, energy, time, social impact). Prioritize:

- Anti-waste & circularity
- Free-time & human impact
- Reusability & no-bulk logic
- Optimization-first design

2. Delivery decision checklist (summary)
---------------------------------------
Use `docs/delivery_decision_checklist.md` for the full process; in short: collect site inputs (density, distance, depot proximity, road quality, regulatory constraints, latency), score and weigh factors, then compare candidate modes. Mixed-mode is often optimal.

3. Packaging guidance (summary)
--------------------------------
- Replace cardboard where practical with modular crates, padded inserts, meal trays (250 ml, 500 ml, 1 L) and bulk bins (20–50 L).
- Materials: PP default for pilots; 316 stainless for industrial/healthcare; anodized aluminum where weight-critical.
- Cleaning: centralized CIP rack-wash for depots; manual distributed cleaning for small hubs.
- Tracking: RFID/QR and deposit/incentive systems.

4. Vehicle modularity (summary)
------------------------------
- Start with depot-level quick-swap modules and standardized racks.
- Consider telescoping crossmembers, sliding side panels, fold-out plates only for controlled low-speed zones.
- Vehicle-adapts-to-cargo: adjustable decks, T-track fixture rails, inflatable supports, and robotic reconfigurers for advanced pilots.

5. Man-hours savings & workforce transition (summary)
--------------------------------------------------
- Typical per-person weekly savings example: ~9.75 hours/week (varies by baseline assumptions).
- Track FTE displacement and creation, retraining outcomes, and distributional effects.
- Include workforce transition budgets and job-pathway programs in pilot financing.

6. Next steps & procurement pointers
-----------------------------------
- Use the delivery decision checklist to pick pilot mode.
- If piloting quickly, procure PP crates and meal trays with silicone gaskets and collapsible return crates; build a depot CIP plan.
- Prototype vehicle modularity with bolt-on rails and removable inserts before telescoping or robotic systems.
