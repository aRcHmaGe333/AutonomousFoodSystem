Contextual notes and provenance (important points found in name-talk)
-----------------------------------------------------------------

- Origin notes: the name-talk file documents that core ideas and much of the early design were developed during a Stockholm University Master programme course (Design for Creative and Immersive Technologies) and linked to a VR critique project about recycling. Preserve this provenance in `not_public/` archives if needed.
- The VR piece and the lived experience (milk/cream panic, Swedish recycling frustrations) shaped the practical constraints: minimal in-home footprint, single-container returns, and block-level provisioning.
- Technical insight flagged in the notes: counterweighted elevator / mass-balancing for gravity-assist transport; mini-maglev for horizontal neighborhood distribution; staged rollout (street-level vehicles and reusable containers → subsurface/elevated infrastructure).
- Social design insight: system intentionally 'takes the choice away' for waste sorting to remove cognitive burden; design for people who are time-poor and spatially constrained (20 m² apartments example).
- Policy critique: the notes contain strong critique of existing recycling systems (example: Swedish seven-bin approach) and recommend design-first infrastructure fixes rather than citizen-level burden.
