# External Review Question Sheet

## Questions for every reviewer

1. Which assumptions look physically or operationally credible?
2. Which assumptions are missing key constraints?
3. Which assumptions are likely to fail in practice?
4. Which risks are currently under-described?
5. What evidence would you require before stronger public or investment-facing claims are justified?

## Dairy engineering questions

- Is passive positioning by corridor geometry and hoof indexing plausible enough to reduce sensing and actuation complexity materially?
- Which tolerances in positioning, lift alignment, and milking coupling are likely unrealistic?
- Which components are most likely to dominate maintenance burden or failure rate?
- What prototype sequence would be required before this direction becomes technically credible?
- Which corridor angles, widths, hoof-indexing constraints, and teat-positioning tolerances would you require before treating the geometry claims as serious?
- Which cradle pressure limits or pinch-point risks would most likely invalidate the current mechanical framing?

## Welfare and animal-behavior questions

- How likely is voluntary or low-resistance entry under the proposed handling assumptions?
- Which elements are most likely to create stress, agitation, avoidance, or injury risk?
- Which welfare claims are directionally reasonable, and which are overstated?
- Which welfare metrics would have to be measured in a pilot?
- How dependent would the concept likely be on reward-feed design or training protocol, and where is that dependency being understated?
- Which refusal-rate or stress-threshold measurements would quickly falsify the calmer-handling premise?

## Biosecurity and food-safety questions

- Is the clean-zone versus biological-zone separation meaningful in practice?
- Where are the highest contamination risks in the handling and milking flow?
- Which sanitation assumptions are too idealized?
- What minimum verification instrumentation would be needed in a real pilot?
- Which sanitation chemistry, contact-time, rinse, and verification details are currently too vague to evaluate credibly?
- What ATP, CFU, or contamination-transfer measurements would be the minimum serious pilot threshold?

## Digestion and waste questions

- Are the digestion and nutrient-loop assumptions even directionally reasonable?
- Which physical constraints are missing from the waste-loop framing?
- What measurements would be required before any loop-efficiency claims could be treated seriously?
- What mass-balance, methane-yield, pathogen-reduction, and nutrient-polishing data would you require before digestate usefulness claims should remain public?

## Economics and operations questions

- What staffing and service model would you assume for a first pilot?
- Which assumptions around anomaly-driven intervention are too optimistic?
- Where is the likely cost cliff in the concept?
- Under what conditions would this concept remain interesting enough to validate further?
- What intervention-rate, maintenance-hour, throughput, and opex-per-liter ranges would make this concept non-credible versus still worth piloting?
