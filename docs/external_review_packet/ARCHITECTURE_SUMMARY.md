# Architecture Summary

## Main operating surfaces

The broader system concept contains four interacting surfaces:

- controlled crop production
- cooking and portioning
- dairy-first livestock handling and milking
- shared waste and resource loops

The external review target in this packet is the dairy-first livestock surface and its interfaces.

## Dairy-first livestock subsystem outline

The livestock concept is organized into six functional blocks:

1. Corridor
2. Cradle or milking-positioning mechanism
3. Pre-wash or pre-entry cleaning stage
4. Sanitation and verification cycle
5. Waste and digestion loop
6. Biosecurity and zone separation

## Intended operational sequence

1. Animal enters a guided corridor.
2. Geometry reduces positioning uncertainty.
3. Pre-wash or stimulation occurs before milking.
4. A cradle-style mechanism supports milking alignment.
5. Milking occurs under simpler sensing and control assumptions than a full robotic-arm approach.
6. Sanitation cycle runs after use.
7. Waste is collected, handled, and potentially routed into digestion and nutrient-loop processes.

## Boundary assumptions

- electronics and sensitive controls should remain separated from the dirtiest biological surfaces
- sanitation must be treated as a first-class subsystem, not an afterthought
- welfare and handling behavior are practical system constraints, not public-relations language
- waste handling is part of the system architecture, not a downstream externality

## What needs external judgment most

- whether the geometry assumptions are realistic
- whether the milking interaction is mechanically viable
- whether sanitation and contamination control are being underestimated
- whether the handling model is welfare-compatible in practice
- whether the cost and intervention assumptions are remotely plausible
