# Dairy-First Pilot Roadmap

Status: Pilot-planning document

Purpose

- Turn the current dairy-first critique into a concrete validation path.
- Define what has to be measured before stronger physical, welfare, sanitation, waste-loop, or economics claims are justified.
- Keep the next step evidence-oriented instead of concept-oriented.

Reading rule

- This is a pilot-planning document, not proof that the livestock concept works.
- The thresholds below are provisional decision gates derived from current critique.
- Failing a gate does not mean the broader project fails. It means the current livestock framing needs correction before stronger claims continue.

## Current posture

The dairy-first subsystem is still best read as:

- software-supported
- simulation-guided
- physically unvalidated
- ready for narrow pilot design, not broad deployment claims

## What this pilot must answer

The first pilot is not trying to prove the whole food-system vision.
It is trying to answer five narrower questions:

1. Can corridor geometry and passive or semi-passive indexing reduce positional variance enough to make the milking interaction worth pursuing?
2. Can the handling model stay inside a defensible welfare envelope rather than relying on optimistic behavior assumptions?
3. Can sanitation and zone separation hold up under repeated use without collapsing throughput?
4. Can the waste and digestion path produce a plausible mass-balance story instead of remaining a narrative convenience?
5. Can intervention rate, maintenance burden, and opex remain inside a range that keeps continued validation rational?

## Pilot sequence

### Phase 0 - Preconditions

Do not start physical pilot work until these are explicit:

- corridor geometry assumptions with provisional angle, width, hoof-indexing, and teat-alignment bounds
- cradle pressure and pinch-point assumptions with mechanical safety review
- reward-feed and training protocol assumptions
- sanitation chemistry, contact time, rinse behavior, and verification method
- waste-loop mass-balance assumptions and digestate handling path
- staffing, anomaly-rate, maintenance-hour, throughput, and opex assumptions for the pilot envelope

Expected output:

- frozen pilot assumptions sheet
- measurement schema
- stop, hold, and redesign criteria

### Phase 1 - Bench and single-animal rig

Goal:

- attack geometry, alignment, and contact-risk assumptions before throughput claims enter the conversation

Focus:

- corridor positioning variance
- teat-alignment repeatability
- cradle contact behavior
- pinch-point and pressure mapping

Expected output:

- geometry variance record
- alignment success record
- mechanical failure and hazard list

### Phase 2 - Supervised low-throughput animal pilot

Goal:

- test whether the handling and sanitation assumptions survive repeated cycles with real animals

Focus:

- refusal rate and agitation indicators
- attachment success and intervention triggers
- sanitation cycle duration and verification results
- contamination transfer checks

Expected output:

- welfare observation log
- sanitation verification log
- intervention and failure log

### Phase 3 - Short operational pilot envelope

Goal:

- determine whether the concept remains interesting once maintenance, staffing, and waste-loop realities are included

Focus:

- throughput per hour
- anomaly and intervention rate
- maintenance hours per cycle block
- methane yield, volatile-solids destruction, pathogen reduction, and digestate profile
- opex per liter and staffing burden

Expected output:

- pilot operating envelope
- continue, redesign, or stop recommendation

## Measurement gates

| Area | Minimum evidence to collect | Provisional gate for continued validation |
|---|---|---|
| Geometry and alignment | positional variance before and after corridor shaping; attachment success; alignment time | variance reduction is measurable and attachment does not depend on hidden manual correction |
| Welfare | refusal rate; agitation markers; stress indicators; abrasion or hoof issues | refusal and stress stay low enough that the calmer-handling premise remains defensible |
| Sanitation | ATP or CFU verification; contamination-transfer checks; cycle duration | sanitation verification passes repeatedly without destroying operational cadence |
| Waste and digestion | manure input estimate; methane yield; volatile-solids destruction; pathogen reduction; digestate nutrient profile | waste-loop story survives a basic mass-balance and does not rely on hand-waving |
| Operations economics | intervention rate; maintenance hours; staffing time; throughput; opex per liter | the pilot remains plausibly worth another iteration instead of obviously uneconomic |

## Suggested instrumentation

- positional measurement for corridor and teat-alignment variance
- cycle timing and attachment logging
- pressure or contact mapping on cradle interfaces
- refusal and agitation observation logging, plus selected welfare indicators
- ATP or CFU sanitation verification tooling
- contamination-transfer test procedure
- digester and digestate sampling workflow
- maintenance and operator intervention log with timestamps

## Hold or redesign triggers

Pause and redesign the concept if any of the following become persistent:

- positional variance remains too wide for repeatable attachment without hidden robotic complexity
- refusal, agitation, or contact-risk indicators contradict the lower-stress premise
- sanitation only works by adding unrealistic labor, chemistry burden, or cycle time
- waste-loop claims fail a basic pilot-scale mass balance
- intervention rate or maintenance burden makes the pilot-envelope economics obviously non-viable

## Evidence required before stronger outward claims

Do not strengthen public or investor-facing wording on the dairy-first subsystem until the pilot produces at least:

- repeatable geometry and attachment evidence
- a welfare observation record that does not contradict the handling claims
- sanitation verification results under repeated use
- a basic waste-loop mass balance with digestate characterization
- operating-envelope numbers for intervention, maintenance, throughput, staffing, and opex

## Practical next steps

1. Freeze the current outward assumptions into one pilot-ready revision.
2. Ask named external reviewers to attack the pilot plan, not just the concept narrative.
3. Convert reviewer objections into pass, fail, and redesign gates before any physical build claims widen.
